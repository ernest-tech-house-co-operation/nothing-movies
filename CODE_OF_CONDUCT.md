# Code of Conduct

Nothing Movies is a small, community-driven project. This document sets
expectations for how people treat each other while contributing, reporting
issues, or discussing the project — it's about behavior, not code or legal
terms (those live in `CONTRIBUTING.md`, `LICENSE.md`, and
`MOVIE_SOURCE_LICENSE.md`).

---

## Our standard

Be someone people want to collaborate with. That's the whole standard,
practically speaking. More specifically:

**Do:**
- Be respectful, even in disagreement
- Give constructive feedback on code, not personal feedback on people
- Assume good faith — most mistakes are mistakes, not malice
- Be patient with newer contributors and people still learning C++/Qt
- Accept that maintainers may reject a PR or idea, and that's not personal

**Don't:**
- Harass, insult, or demean anyone in issues, PRs, discussions, or any
  project space
- Make discriminatory remarks based on someone's identity, background,
  skill level, or anything else
- Spam issues/PRs, or use them to promote unrelated projects or content
- Deliberately submit bad-faith contributions (e.g. a movie source you
  know violates `MOVIE_SOURCE.md`, just to cause friction or make a point)
- Publicly disclose a security vulnerability before it's been reported
  and fixed per `SECURITY.md`

---

## Scope

This applies everywhere the project has a presence: GitHub issues, pull
requests, discussions, and any official communication channel (email,
Discord, etc. if/when one exists).

---

## Reporting a problem

If someone's behavior violates this code of conduct, email
**ernesttechhouse@gmail.com** with details. Reports are handled privately,
and we won't publicly name someone based on an unconfirmed report.

---

## Enforcement

Maintainers decide what action fits the situation. Depending on severity
and whether it's a first occurrence, this can range from:

1. A private warning and a request to adjust behavior
2. A public warning on the relevant issue/PR
3. Temporary restriction from contributing (e.g. PRs closed without review
   for a period)
4. Permanent ban from the project's spaces for repeated or severe
   violations

There's no fixed formula — context matters, and maintainers use judgment
the same way they do for reviewing movie source submissions.

---

## One practical note

This project deals with a genuinely difficult subject area (see
`WARNING.md`) — sourcing, torrenting, scraping. Debate about what sources
should or shouldn't be accepted, or what features are appropriate, is fine
and expected. Keep that debate about the project and its direction, not
about the people in it.
